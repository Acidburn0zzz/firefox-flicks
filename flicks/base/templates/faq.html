{{ _('Firefox Flicks FAQ') }}

{{ _('What is Firefox Flicks?') }}

{% trans %} Firefox Flicks is a contest that invites Firefox fans and filmmakers from all over the world to create short videos (or "flicks") to help tell the Firefox story. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('When will the contest open?') }}

{% trans %} The Firefox Flicks contest site is now open. You can now upload your video and vote on your favorites. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Can I submit more than one flick?') }}

{% trans %} You can submit as many as you like, but you have to submit a new entry each time. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Can a team develop the flick?') }}

{% trans %} Of course! Teams can be up to 10 people. We will, however, be sending out a maximum of one Firefox prize pack per submission to be shared with the team. Also, please appoint a team leader. All communication will be with this person and only one prize will be awarded per entry. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Is the contest open to international entries?') }}

{% trans %} Yes. This contest is open to anyone in world. We will, however, choose one winner for North America, one winner for Latin America, one winner for Africa, Australia & New Zealand and one winner for Europe (or whichever of those is closest to where you're from). {% endtrans %}

{{ _('What are the judging criteria?') }}

{% trans %} Videos will be judged on quality of shooting, editing and how closely they align with the creative brief. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Entries will be judged in the following categories:') }}

  1. {{ _('Best :30 Spot') }}
  2. {{ _('Best Animation') }}
  3. {{ _('Best Use of New Technology (e.g. Popcorn.js, shot entirely with a mobile device, 3D)') }}
  4. {{ _('Best Public Service Announcement') }}

{% trans %} A panel of judges will choose the grand prize winner from a selection of videos that have been voted on by the public. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Who are the judges?') }}

{% trans %} See our Judges page for a complete list. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('What can I win?') }}

{% trans %} Simply by competing, your flick will be seen and voted on by millions of Firefox fans around the world, giving you exposure to a global audience. You'll also get your entry in front of international advertising executives and film luminaries while working on one of the world's biggest brands — a great piece to add to any portfolio. {% endtrans %}

{% trans %} But there's more! The grand prize winner in each region (North America, Latin America, EU and Asia, Australia & New Zealand) will receive their choice of either $10,000 (USD) in video equipment or cash to spend on film school expenses or your next project. There will be four category winners in each of the four regions, the winners will get $5,000 (USD) in video equipment or cash to spend on film school expenses or your next project. There will also be runner ups in each of the four categories in each region, these winners will receive $1,000 (USD) in video equipment or cash to spend on film school expenses or your next project. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Is there a maximum video length?') }}

{% trans %} The maximum video length is 120 seconds. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Who can I contact if I have additional questions on the contest?') }}

{% trans flicks_mail='mailto:firefoxflicks@mozilla.org' %} Drop us a note at firefoxflicks@mozilla.org and we'll get back to you as soon as we can. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Can I submit an entry as a university project?') }}

{% trans %} Absolutely! We think that Firefox Flicks is a great opportunity for students to participate in a real world, high visibility campaign with expert judges from the film, television and advertising industries. Talk to your professors about using the video as your project. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Can I use the Firefox wordmark and logo in my flick?') }}

{% trans trademark='http://www.mozilla.org/foundation/trademarks/policy.html', wordmark='http://mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/brand/' %} Yes. For the purposes of this contest, you may use the Firefox wordmark and logo. For more information, see the Mozilla Trademark Policy. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('How do I get high resolution Firefox name and logo artwork?') }}

{% trans brand='http://mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/brand/' %} To get high resolution copies of the Firefox name and logo artwork, as well as guidance on how to use the Firefox logo and wordmarks, please visit our Brand Toolkit. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Where can I find music or other materials to include in my flick?') }}

{% trans creative_commons='http://search.creativecommons.org', public_domain='http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_domain' %} If you're looking for additional materials — whether music, video footage, artwork or graphics — we suggest you investigate public domain media.Creative Commons has a nice search service for locating public domain work. {{ _('Can I use third-party trademarks or copyrighted materials in my flick?') }}

{% trans %} No, not without permission from the owner of those materials. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Can I submit a flick in a language other than English?') }}

{% trans flicks_email='mailto:firefoxflicks@mozilla.org' %} Yes. For non-English entries, you will need to submit a full and accurate script or subtitles. If you're having trouble getting your flick translated, please contact us. We can help. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('What if I want to withdraw my submission?') }}

{% trans flicks_email='mailto:firefoxflicks@mozilla.org' %} If you would like to withdraw your submission, please contact us at firefoxflicks@mozilla.org and we will remove your entry from the competition. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('What aspect ratio should I use for my entry?') }}

{% trans %} All aspect ratios will be accepted, but 16:9 is recommended and preferred. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Will entries be available to the general public?') }}

{% trans %} Your flick will be posted on the voting site so that people can watch and upvote their favorite flicks. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('What is a PSA?') }}

{{ _('What is a PSA?') }} {% trans %} A public service announcement (PSA) is a type of advertisement that intends to affect public attitudes by raising awareness of an issue and possibly inspiring action. If entering this category, your video should focus more on the issues that affect people and their online lives, including privacy and security, and less about Firefox specifically. {% endtrans %}
