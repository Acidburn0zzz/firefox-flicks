Firefox Flicks Contest Official Rules

Introduction: The objective of this Contest is to give budding film makers the opportunity to create and submit short videos promoting Firefox OS. The contest will run worldwide (except for the excluded countries below) and winners will be chosen from four regions around the world - (i) the U.S. and Canada, (ii) Latin America, (iii) Europe, Middle East and Africa, and (iv) Asia, Australia and New Zealand.

Sponsor: The sponsor of this Contest is Mozilla Corporation, located at 650 Castro Street, Suite 300, Mountain View, CA 94041 ("Sponsor").

Eligibility:
No purchase or fee is necessary to enter or claim any prize.

The Contest is open to individuals who are at least the age of majority in their country of residence (18 in most countries).

The Contest is open to individual filmmakers and teams of up to three (3) individuals. Teams must pick a team leader, who will submit the team's video and be the sole recipient of any prizes for the team (in other words there is only one prize per winning video, not a prize for each member of the team) except for the Grand Prize (more information in the Prizes section below).

The Contest is void in Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Sudan, Syria, and any other country subject to a U.S boycott or where prohibited by law or restricted by law or regulations.

Employees of Sponsor and its parent company, subsidiaries, affiliates, suppliers, prize providers, advertising agencies, and the families of and persons living in the same household as such employees, are not eligible to participate.

By entering you agree to be bound by these Official Rules and all decisions of the Sponsor and judges, which are final and binding. The Contest is subject to all applicable laws.

How to Enter: To enter the Contest, log into https://firefoxflicks.mozilla.org (the “Contest Site”) with your Persona credentials, fill in the requested information, and follow the instructions to submit your video ("Entry"). Your Entry will be processed by Vimeo and posted to both the Contest Site and the Vimeo site under a special Firefox Flicks account. Please review the Vimeo Terms of Service and Privacy Policy. As part of the submission process, you will need to provide your name, give your Entry a title, provide a brief description of the Entry, and select your country of residence.

If you are a team of individuals submitting an Entry, the Entry should be submitted under the email account of the team leader. All the individuals on the team should be identified in some way in the description for proper attribution.

You must be the named and authorized account holder of the email account through which your Entry is submitted. The authorized account holder is the natural person whose information was submitted to the email provider when the account was created. Entries submitted will not be returned or acknowledged.

Without limiting any other rights, the Sponsor has the right to, in the Sponsor's sole discretion, reject or remove from the Contest any Entry that, in the Sponsor's sole discretion, violates these Rules, any applicable Sponsor policy, any applicable laws, or is in any way harmful or objectionable. Sponsor also reserves the right to disqualify any entrant or Entry at the Sponsor's sole discretion. No correspondence shall be entered into.

Privacy: In order to enter the Contest, you will be required to provide your name, your email address (through your Persona login), and your country of residence (to determine your Contest region) at the time of submission. Your name and region will be posted along with your Entry in the public contest galleries on the Contest Site and the Vimeo site, where they can be viewed, liked, and shared by everyone. Any entrant who wishes to receive the surprise thank you prize pack will also need to provide a mailing address. Winners will be asked to provide a mailing address so that we can send them their prizes and for general contact purposes related to the Contest. If you check the box to be notified about Firefox Flicks information and events, we will send you periodic email updates about this and future Firefox Flicks contests. Except as otherwise stated in these rules or as otherwise agreed by you and the Sponsor, email and mailing addresses and other personal information you provide in connection with the Contest will not be made public or shared, except with vendors providing contest related services to Sponsor, and will be used only for purposes relating to this Contest, and in accordance with our Privacy Policy located at http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/privacy-policy.html. The Vimeo privacy policy is available at http://www.vimeo.com/privacy. The data you provide about yourself may be transferred from your country to the U.S. or other countries.

Contest Period: The Contest begins at 3:00 p.m. Pacific Standard Time (PST) (11:00 p.m. GMT) on Wednesday, February 13, 2013 and ends at 11:59 p.m. PST on Wednesday, July 31, 2013 (7:59 a.m. GMT on August 1, 2013) (the "Contest Period"). Judging will occur throughout the Contest Period for Early Entry Prizes (described below), as well as immediately following the Contest Period for Regional Prizes and the Grand Prize as described below. Following the Contest Period there will also be People’s Choice voting (described below), which will open at 12:01 a.m. PST (8:01 a.m. GMT) on August 1, 2013 and end at 11:59 p.m. PST on August 31, 2013 (7:59 a.m. GMT on September 1, 2013) (the “Voting Period”).

Work Criteria: All Entries must meet specific criteria to be accepted into the Contest. Without limitation, the following conditions apply: (a) The Entry must comply with the creative brief provided by Sponsor. (b) The Entry must be submitted under the Creative Commons Attribution - NonCommercial - ShareAlike license. (c) The Entry must be your original work and may not include any unauthorized intellectual property of a third party (such as a video clip, music, or visible brand name or logo you do not have a license to use). (d) You may use the graphics and logos supplied by Sponsor. Such use must comply with the Mozilla Trademark Policy (http://www.mozilla.org/foundation/trademarks/) and the Firefox Brand Toolkit (http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/brand/). Mozilla retains all ownership in such graphics and logos. (e) The Entry may be no longer than 60 seconds. (f) Non-English language videos must have English subtitles or a written English translation included. (g) The Entry must be in a format that can be uploaded to Vimeo (see http://vimeo.com/help/compression). (h) The description of the Entry included in your submission must be clear and accurate. (i) The Entry must not be obscene, libelous, or defamatory, violate the right of privacy or publicity of any third party, or violate any applicable laws, and must comply with Mozilla's legal notices and policies. See http://www.mozilla.com/en-US/about/legal.html. (j) If anyone not on the submitting team appears in the video, you must obtain their explicit permission to videotape them, to enter the video in this Contest, and to give Mozilla the right to freely use and distribute the video. We also recommend adding closed captioning to your Entries.

Selection of Winners: Winners will be selected for Early Entry Prizes (described below) three times during the Contest in conjunction with International Film Festivals. One winner will be selected for each Early Entry mini contest. The winners will be selected by a knowledgeable panel of Mozilla staffers. The top three Entries selected by the Mozilla judges for each Early Entry mini contest are guaranteed to be submitted for judging by the film industry judging panel that will select the Regional Prize winners and the Grand Prize winner. After the Contest Period has ended, the Mozilla judges will select the best thirty-one (31) Entries not already Early Entry mini contest selections and these 31 Entries plus the top three Entries from each Early Entry mini contest (9 total) will be reviewed and judged by a panel of qualified judges from the film industry who will select the Regional Prize winners, and together with Panasonic, the Grand Prize winner. There will be a winner and a runner-up from each of the four geographical regions identified below and one Grand Prize winner. Your country of residence, which you select at the time of Entry, determines your Entry’s region.

In order to encourage submission of Entries early in the Contest Period, there will be three (3) Early Entry mini contests. The Mozilla judges will review all Entries submitted by the entry deadline for each mini contest and will select one winner for each mini contest. They will also select two other top entries to be submitted along with the winner for judging by the expert film industry judging panel. The deadline for each Early Entry mini contest will be announced on the Contest Site.

The regions from which regional winners will be chosen are (i) the U.S. and Canada, (ii) Latin America, (iii) Europe, Middle East and Africa (EMEA), and (iv) Asia, , Australia and New Zealand (APAC). All countries, except those not eligible, are intended to fall into one of the four regions. All Entries will be judged on the following equally weighted criteria: quality of shooting, quality of editing, creativity, and how closely they align with the Contest creative brief. The decisions of the judges are final on all matters relating to the Contest and no correspondence will be entered into.

People’s Choice Winners: In addition to the winners selected by the judges described above, there will be three People’s Choice winners who will be selected by public voting. In order to vote, you must sign into the Contest Site using your Persona Credentials and use the voting mechanism provided on the Contest Site to cast no more than one vote per Entry during the Voting Period. Limit one vote per person and per email address per Entry. If more than one vote is received from the same person or email address for the same Entry, only the first such vote received will be deemed valid. Any votes generated by script, macro, multiple email addresses, aliases, use of contest services, payoffs or promises to others in exchange for votes, or anything else that is inconsistent with getting an honest picture of the public’s genuine opinion of your Entry, is grounds for immediate disqualification of the person submitting (or inducing) such voting, as well as the Entry that is the subject of such activity. Sponsor reserves the right, in its sole and absolute discretion, to determine which forms for voting activity may be deemed to violate the spirit of this rule and therefore to be invalid, and also to disqualify and exclude from this Contest and any other contest Sponsor may offer any person(s) engaged in activity that violates any provision of these Rules or is otherwise deemed by Sponsor, in its discretion, to be inappropriate, unsportsmanlike or disruptive to the integrity or intended functioning of this Contest. All votes are final and cannot be revoked. The three Entries receiving the highest numbers of valid votes from the public, as determined by the Sponsor, will be deemed the winners of the People’s Choice vote, subject to verification of the entrants’ eligibility and compliance with these Official Rules. In case of a tie, Sponsor shall make the final decision.

Prizes: There are three (3) Early Entry mini contest winners, four (4) Regional Prize winners, four (4) Regional Prize runners-up, and one (1) Grand Prize winner. The winner of each Early Entry mini contest will receive $1,000 and a selection of Mozilla swag. The Regional winners (1 from each region) will each receive $5,000 and the Regional runners-up (1 from each region) will each receive $2,500. One (1) Grand Prize winner will receive a trip to Los Angeles, California to professionally produce their winning Entry with help from Hollywood insiders and representatives from Panavision using professional Panavision equipment. If a team created the winning Entry, the entire team (up to 3 members total) will be flown to LA to participate in the prize experience. The travel portion of the Grand Prize consists of roundtrip coach air travel for each winning team member between a major airport nearest that team member’s home and Los Angeles, California and 5 nights’ hotel accommodations at a moderately priced hotel. Winners must travel on dates specified by the Sponsor. A major credit card will be required upon hotel check-in to cover any incidental expenses. The winners are responsible for possessing all necessary passports or other travel documents. Failure of a team member to be available or able to travel on the designated dates may result in forfeit of the prize by that team member. If an individual winner or all members of a winning team are unable to travel on the designated dates, if time permits, an alternate winner may be selected. While the value of the experience of working with experienced professionals cannot be estimated in dollars, the estimated retail value of the travel portion of the prize is approximately $10,000. Actual value will vary depending on the number of team members, departure cities, and other factors. If a winner resides in the Los Angeles area, ground transportation will be substituted for air. Any difference between actual and stated value will not be awarded. All travel and accommodations are subject to availability and Sponsor discretion.

In addition to the above prizes, all entrants who choose to provide their mailing address will receive a Firefox Flicks prize pack. In the case of a team, the individual who submitted the Entry will receive the prize pack.

Three People’s Choice winners will be selected by public vote. The People’s Choice winners will receive exposure through Mozilla channels but will not win any prize with significant monetary value.

General Prize Conditions: The winners are solely responsible for the payment of any taxes that may arise as a result of acceptance of a prize, and all other expenses or costs relating to the prize not expressly set forth above. Prizes cannot be transferred or substituted and must be accepted as awarded. Sponsor reserves the right to substitute a prize of equal or greater value in its sole discretion if an advertised prize becomes unavailable. Please note that if the winning Entry was submitted by a team, only one prize will be awarded to the team. A prize will not be given to each member of the team, except in the case of the Grand Prize, in which all team members (up to 3) will be allowed to participate in the prize experience.

Winner Notification/Validation: The winners of the Regional Prizes and Grand Prize will be notified by email on or about August 23, 2013. The winners of the Early Entry mini contests will be notified within seven (7) days following the submission deadline for the mini contest. The People’s Choice winners will be notified on or about September 3, 2013. If a winner has not responded within five (5) days after Sponsor has sent the notification, or in the event of non-compliance with any of these requirements, the prize may be forfeited, and the entrant having the next highest score will be deemed the winner. The winner, and all team members if the winning entry was submitted by a team, may be required to sign and return to Sponsor an affidavit of eligibility and publicity release, where lawful, within ten (10) days of the date of winning notification. The Regional Prize, Grand Prize, and People’s Choice winners will be announced at the Toronto Film Festival between September 5th and 15th, 2013 during a Mozilla hosted event. The Early Entry mini contest winners will be announced at film festivals taking place during the Contest Period.
