Firefox Flicks Contest Official Rules

Introduction: The objective of this Contest is to give budding film makers the opportunity to create and submit short videos promoting Firefox. The contest will run worldwide (except for the excluded countries below) and winners will be chosen from four regions around the world — (i) the U.S. and Canada, (ii) Latin America, (iii) Europe, and (iv) Asia, Africa, Australia and New Zealand.

Sponsor: The sponsor of this Contest is Mozilla Corporation, located at 650 Castro Street, Suite 300, Mountain View, CA 94041 ("Sponsor").

Eligibility: No purchase or fee is necessary to enter or claim any prize.

The Contest is open to individuals who are at least the age of majority in their country of residence. Minors 13 years or older who reside in the U.S. and minors in certain other countries depending on local laws are also eligible to participate in the Contest but will be required to submit a signed parental consent form with their Entry. Email us at ecompton@mozilla.com for a parental consent form.

The Contest is open to individual developers and teams of up to ten (10) individuals. Teams must pick a team leader, who will submit the team's video and be the sole recipient of any prizes for the team (in other words there is only one prize per winning video, not a prize for each member of the team).

The Contest is void in Cuba, Iran, Myanmar, North Korea, Sudan, Syria, and any other country subject to a U.S boycott or where prohibited by law or restricted by law or regulations.

Employees of Sponsor and its parent company, subsidiaries, affiliates, suppliers, advertising agencies, and the families of and persons living in the same household as such employees, are not eligible to participate.

By entering you agree to be bound by these Official Rules and all decisions of the Sponsor and judges, which are final and binding. The Contest is subject to all applicable laws.

How to Enter: To enter the Contest, log into http://firefoxflicks.mozilla.org, fill in your profile information, and follow the instructions to submit your video ("Entry"). You will need to provide your country of residence and select the judging category to which you are submitting your Entry. You may submit as many Entries as you want. Your Entries will be posted to a contest video gallery on the Vidly site (http://www.vid.ly).

If you are a team of individuals submitting an Entry, the Entry should be submitted under the email account of the team leader. All the individuals on the team should be identified in some way in the description for proper attribution.

You must be the named and authorized account holder of the email account through which your Entry is submitted. The authorized account holder is the natural person whose information was submitted to the email provider when the account was created. Entries submitted will not be returned or acknowledged.

Without limiting any other rights, the Sponsor has the right to, in the Sponsor's sole discretion, reject or remove from the Contest any Entry that, in the Sponsor's sole discretion, violates these Rules, any applicable Sponsor policy, any applicable laws, or is in any way harmful or objectionable. Sponsor also reserves the right to disqualify any entrant or Entry at the Sponsor's sole discretion. No correspondence shall be entered into.

Privacy: Entrants will be required to provide their name, email address, and country at the time of submission. Name, region, and video category will be posted along with each video in the contest gallery on the Vidly site (http://www.vid.ly). Entrants may also provide a website address, biographical information, and/or an avatar if they wish to have those included in their public profile in the contest gallery. Any entrant who wishes to receive the surprise thank you prize pack will also need to provide a mailing address. Winners will be asked to provide a mailing address so that we can send them their prizes and for general contact purposes related to the Contest. All Entries and the entrants' profiles will be public on a contest website hosted by Vidly, where they can be viewed, liked, and shared by everyone. Except as otherwise stated in these rules or as otherwise agreed by you and the Sponsor, email and mailing addresses and other personal information you provide in connection with the Contest will not be made public or shared, except with vendors providing contest related services to Sponsor, and will be used only for purposes relating to this Contest, and in accordance with our Privacy Policy located at http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/privacy-policy.html. The Vidly service privacy policy is available at http://www.encoding.com/privacy. The data you provide about yourself may be transferred from your country to the U.S. or other countries.

Contest Period: The Contest begins at 12:01 a.m. Pacific Standard Time (PST) (8:01 a.m. GMT) on Tuesday, February 28, 2012 and ends at 11:59 p.m. PST on Tuesday, May 1, 2012 (7:59 a.m. GMT on May 2 It will be immediately followed by the judging, as described below.

Work Criteria: All Entries must meet specific criteria to be accepted into the Contest. Without limitation, the following conditions apply: (a) The Entry must qualify for one of the judging categories, be submitted to the right category, and comply with the creative brief provided by Sponsor. (b) The Entry must be submitted under the Creative Commons Attribution — NonCommercial — ShareAlike license. (c) The Entry must be your original work and may not include any unauthorized intellectual property of a third party (such as a video clip, music, or visible brand name or logo you do not have a license to use). (d) You may use the graphics and logos supplied by Sponsor subject to the Mozilla Trademark Policy ( http://www.mozilla.org/foundation/trademarks/) and the Firefox Brand Toolkit (http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/brand/). Mozilla retains all ownership in such graphics and logos. (e) The Entry may be no longer than 120 seconds. (f) Non-English language videos must have English subtitles included. (g) The Entry must be in a format that can be uploaded to Vidly. (h) The description of the Entry included in your submission must be clear and accurate. (i) The Entry must not be obscene, libelous, or defamatory, violate the right of privacy or publicity of any third party, or violate any applicable laws, and must comply with Mozilla's legal notices and policies. See http://www.mozilla.com/en-US/about/legal.html. (j) If anyone not on the submitting team appears in the video, you must obtain their explicit permission to videotape them, to enter the video in this Contest, and to give Mozilla the right to freely use and distribute the video.

Selection of Winners: After the Contest Period has ended, all Entries will be reviewed and judged by a panel of qualified judges from the film industry who will select the winners. There will be two winners in each of the four categories listed below from each of the four geographical regions identified below, and one grand prize winner from each region, for a total of 36 winners. The categories from which winners will be selected are: best 30 second spot, best public service announcement, best animation, and best use of open technologies (e.g: Popcorn.js, HTML5). Entrants select the category in which they want their Entry judged at the time of submission. The regions from which winners will be chosen are (i) the U.S. and Canada, (ii) Latin America, (iii) Europe, and (iv) Asia, Africa, Australia and New Zealand. All countries, except those not eligible, will fall into one of the four regions. Sponsor will determine which region a country falls into in its sole discretion. Within each category, Entries will be judged on the following equally weighted criteria: quality of shooting, quality of editing, creativity, and how closely they align with the Contest creative brief. The decisions of the judging panel are final on all matters relating to the Contest and no correspondence will be entered into.

Prizes: There are thirty-two (32) category prize winners and four (4) grand prize winners. The category prize winners (2 from each category in each region) will each receive $1,000 worth of video equipment (either Mozilla and the winner will mutually agree upon the specific equipment or Mozilla will provide a gift card to a store that carries video equipment, at Mozilla's sole discretion); provided, however, if a category prize winner is currently enrolled in an accredited film school or film program at an accredited college or university, that winner may elect to receive $1,000 in cash to be used towards school expenses rather than the video equipment. One (1) grand prize winner from each region will receive $5,000 worth of video equipment (either Mozilla and the winner will mutually agree upon the specific equipment or Mozilla will provide a gift card to a store that carries video equipment, at Mozilla's sole discretion); provided, however, if a grand prize winner is currently enrolled in an accredited film school or film program at an accredited college or university, that winner may elect to receive $5,000 in cash to be used towards school expenses rather than the video equipment. If a team created the winning Entry, the prize will be awarded to the individual who submitted the Entry.

In addition to the above prizes, all entrants who choose to provide their mailing address will receive a Firefox Flicks prize pack. In the case of a team, the individual who submitted the Entry will receive the prize pack.

Prizes cannot be transferred or substituted and must be accepted as awarded. Sponsor reserves the right to substitute a prize of equal or greater value in its sole discretion if an advertised prize becomes unavailable. Please note that if the winning Entry was submitted by a team, only one prize will be awarded to the team. A prize will not be given to each member of the team.

Winner Notification/Validation: The winners will be notified by email on or about May 7, 2012. If a winner has not responded within five (5) days after Sponsor has sent the notification, or in the event of non-compliance with any of these requirements, the prize may be forfeited, and the entrant having the next highest score will be deemed the winner. The winner, and all team members if the winning entry was submitted by a team, may be required to sign and return to Sponsor an affidavit of eligibility and publicity release, where lawful, within ten (10) days of the date of winning notification.
